Deep reasoning, strategy, code, fine-tooth-comb analysis. The brain power turned all the way up. Uses the most tokens.

Tell Claude Who You Are

Go into Settings and fill out your personal preferences. This is where you brief your crew before they start any work.

What to Include

  • Who you are and what you do
  • Your brands and target market
  • How you want it to respond (your style)

  • Social media rules - hook, context, story, value, teaching, CTA
  • Email rules - warm, never "I hope this finds you well"
  • Formatting rules - no em dashes, Australian spelling, whatever matters to you

Create Your First Project

Projects give Claude a memory. You put your brand kit in here, your instructions, your context - and every conversation inside that project has all of it. No more re-explaining yourself.

Two Things Go Into a Project

Instructions = The Rules

How you want Claude to behave. Your tone, your style, content rules, email rules, design rules. This is you briefing your crew.

Files = The Knowledge

Reference documents Claude can read - your brand guide, services and pricing, testimonials, product info. Anything you want Claude to have at its fingertips. MD files (markdown - just text) work best.

How We Set It Up

  • Create a new Project - name it for your brand
  • Paste your instructions into the Instructions section
  • Drag your brand guide (MD file) into Files
  • Drag your services and pricing into Files
  • Test it - ask for a social media caption and watch it nail your voice first try
Write a short Instagram caption for [your brand] announcing [your thing]
"Instructions are the rules. Files are the knowledge. That's all a Project is." - Kelly Wotherspoon
Key insight from the session: Chat can't DO anything for you - it's just question and answer. But if you take your Project context into Cowork, then Claude can actually DO things with it. That's the difference.
5

Set Up Your Canva Brand Kit

When Canva knows your brand AND Claude knows your brand, everything that gets created is perfectly on-brand. You do this once and you're set.

Steps

  • Open Canva and go to Brand Kit
  • Create a new Brand Kit - name it your brand name
  • Add your brand colours (hex codes from your brand guide)
  • Add your fonts (display font + body font)
  • Upload your logo
  • Optionally add photos, textures, mood imagery
Branding rule of thumb: Choose 3 colours you love, then pick a light and dark end of the same colour wheel for text overlay. Then add a splash of something unexpected. Go on vibes - find pictures you love and pull colours from them.

Skills: Jobs That Run Themselves

Skills are jobs - end to end processes you want your AI to do. Create it once, run it forever.

How to Create a Skill

Do the job with Claude in Cowork first. Once it's done, say: "That process we just did - turn it into a skill." Claude creates the skill file. Then add it to your Skills in Settings.

Run any skill by typing /skill-name and it executes the entire process end to end.

"When you're creating a new skill, run it through Skill Creator. It's built into Claude to make sure your skills are formatted the way Claude reads them best. It will run perfectly every time." - Kelly Wotherspoon

Skill Ideas

  • Content creation - one command, a week of posts
  • Video to clips - long video in, short clips out
  • Email drafting - read inbox, draft responses in your voice
  • Daily briefing - morning summary of what matters
  • Client prep - research before every call
  • Transcription - audio/video to text

Build Digital Assets and Lead Magnets

"You don't own your social media. You don't own your following. So you've got to get them onto a mailing list. Create digital assets and lead magnets - give away free stuff, but have a gate in front of it so you capture their data." - Kelly Wotherspoon

The Strategy

Create something that makes people go "oh my god I NEED this" and hand over their email. Give it away for free - but gate it. Now they're on YOUR mailing list. When you launch? You email YOUR list. Not some algorithm's list. Yours.

What We Built Live

A Soul Check-In Interactive Wheel for Healing Soul - an interactive self-assessment where users rate themselves across 8 areas of healing and get personalised insights. Built in minutes.

Create a digital lead magnet for [your brand]. Make it a [quiz / interactive tool / PDF guide / journalling kit]. It should capture email, be beautifully branded, and give them a taste of what working with you feels like. Build it as an HTML artifact I can download.

Lead Magnet Ideas

  • Interactive quiz with personalised results
  • 7-day challenge (delivered by email)
  • Branded PDF guide or starter kit
  • Mini audio experience (breathwork, meditation, guided journal)
  • Self-assessment tool with visual results
Pre-launch strategy: Even before your product is live, create branded content and lead magnets. Build a waitlist. Drip-feed them juiciness. Then when you launch, everyone goes nuts. That's how you break your website - in the best way.

Automate with Scheduled Tasks

In Cowork, you can schedule tasks to run automatically. Daily briefings, content creation, email checking - set it and forget it.

Example: Daily Content Run

Schedule a task that runs every Monday morning: create a week of branded posts, save them to your Canva account, and email you a summary. You wake up, review, and post. Or don't even post - let another tool handle that too.

Example: End of Day Summary

Schedule a task that runs every evening: check your emails for anything missed, summarise your calls from today, pull out action items, and update your task list. Ready for tomorrow morning.
